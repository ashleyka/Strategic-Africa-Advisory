Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common compliance and business setup queries in Zambia.

Typically, company incorporation takes between 3 to 5 business days once the company name is approved and all directorship/shareholder signatures are secured. Stratagem Africa manages the entire incorporation workflow to minimize delays.

Every newly registered company must register for a Taxpayer Identification Number (TPIN) with the Zambia Revenue Authority (ZRA). Depending on turnover, you may also need to register for Value Added Tax (VAT), and Pay As You Earn (PAYE) if employing local staff.

Yes. Foreign nationals and international corporations can own 100% of a local private company in most commercial sectors. Certain highly regulated sectors, like telecommunications or banking, may require local shareholding percentages.

Under the Zambian Companies Act, every company must designate a Company Secretary. They are responsible for filing statutory annual returns, documenting resolutions, maintaining official registers, and ensuring overall governance compliance.
